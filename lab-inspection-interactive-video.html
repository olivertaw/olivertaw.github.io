About Works

SFU Laboratory Safety Inspection

Role: Director, Editor
Type: Interactive Video
Year: 2020

Overview

This interactive training video is for researchers and lab users who wants to get familiar with the SFU’s lab environment before their lab inspection process. This video serves as a visual aid for the existing Lab Inspection Training. By adding choices and interactivity to the video, it increases immersion, enhances learning and retention.

Member(s)

Oliver Taw

Tools

‍Adobe After Effects, Adobe Premiere Pro, Eko Interactive Platform

Process

The initial step for this video was to create a script for our pathways. Since we have existing training, my supervisor referred most of it from that training. After I went over the script, I map out the possible pathways and branches for the video. I used the Eko studio platform to map out multiple pathways for the storyline. After a few revisions of the multi-pathway approach, we decided that the multiple choices and pathways would confuse the trainees and hence we decided to go with a single pathway.

Fig 1: Initial pathways for multiple choices in Eko Studio

As soon as the pathway was laid out, I went into the lab for location scouting. I took some footage for potential shots in the lab as well as the constraints that we could face. I found out that the lab space is a bit tight and didn’t have enough room to move around. After this location scouting, I started to plan out the shots by storyboarding the scenes.

Fig 2: Storyboard of the Lab Inspection Training video

I then went back into the lab for filming and took all the footage as well as B-roll footage. Afterward, I proceed to organize and edit the footage in Adobe Premiere Pro and After Effects. My supervisor provided me the audio recordings and I synced the audio clips with their respective footages. I then color graded the footage using the Lumetri controls and added pop-up texts and transitions. I followed SFU's color and typography guidelines to add the remaining animations and branding.

Fig 3: Adding texts and 2D animation effects in Adobe After Effects

When it comes to interactivity, I decided to add custom interactive buttons from the main elements of each scene. In order to achieve this, I went into the scenes and cut out these objects in Photoshop. Next, I added a glow to these objects so that they are more easily identifiable in the scenes. These pop-up elements were then added to their respective scenes on Eko Platform. After everything was edited and laid out, I arranged the linear pathway for the scenes. This enables the interactive state so that the viewers can interact with it.

Fig 4: Inserting the outlined pop-ups elements into the scene

Reflection

This project is my very first experience with an interactive training video. There were a few challenges along the way such as thinking about how I can connect the scenes and thinking how to make the transitions seamless. I also had to come up with ways to create and edit the interactive elements, and how to put them in each scene. As with any other first-time experience, it was a puzzling experience for me. But the feedback I received was overwhelmingly positive, and I would consider that as learning success. This video is also one of the first-ever interactive training videos in the history of SFU training videos. I've learned so much along the way and I hope that I can create even more immersive and interactive training videos in the future.
