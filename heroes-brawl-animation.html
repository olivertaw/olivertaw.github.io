About Works

Heroes' Brawl

Role: Animator, Modeler
Type: Short Animated Film
Year: 2018

Overview

"Heroes' Brawl" is a short video mixed of 3D animation and film which tells the story about four friends trying out a new game and making fun of each other character's costumes and weapon of choice.

Member(s)

Oliver Taw, Michelle Shen, Laura Ko, Amy Tse

Tools

Maya, Adobe Premiere Pro

Process

I was responsible for the modeling and rigging of the characters. I started to model the body with polygon primitive. I then extruded the wings for my model so that it would be easier for me to rig the model later. For the legs, I used extrusion and insertion of the edge loops to make the borders smoother. As for the beak, I had to model it separately as it is sharp and curvy. The reason for that is it was easier for me to attach the beak's vertices to the main mesh so that they became one overall mesh.

Fig 1: Modeling the character with an image reference

As for the environment, I used the simple planar surface and then used the sculpt tool to lift the surface. I applied the stamp tool using a displacement map for the terrain. I then put in directional light to stimulate the sun for the environment, and a skydome light to illuminate the environment. Afterward, I assembled the scene by bringing in the characters and the props. I then planned out the layout for the characters and moved to the final stages of the animation.

Fig 2: Creating a 3D model of the environment

During the animation phase, I would apply the animation principles to my character in order for him to have more personality. I used the Squash and Stretch, Secondary Action, Anticipating, and Exaggeration principles. For secondary actions, as soon as the bird flies up, his leg moves which gives the scene more life, supporting the main action. When the bird is flapping its wing to fly down, I applied the exaggerated effects to move his wings and feet by placing the keyframes in short intervals. After showing our animatics, we got feedback that some characters lack personality. So, we tried to do research and referenced other animation and real-life animals and gave our characters new idle animations.

Fig 3: Character Animation

Reflection

3D Animation was a very integral part of my life when I was growing up. And after taking this course, I am even more inspired to become a 3D animator. In this course, I was able to learn every aspect of 3D animation production pipeline and had a chance to utilize the skills and principles to produce this video. More importantly, I discovered that I love the character animation aspect and I'm determined to learn and animate more in the future.
