About Works

E-waste
Now And Beyond

Role: Director, Editor
Type: Short Documentary
Year: 2019

Overview

For this short documentary, 4 of us worked in collaboration with a third-party organization and tackled an existing global issue of electronic waste (E-waste) and how people consumed and disposed irresponsibly of the electronics they used

Member(s)

Oliver Taw, Erin Jang, Timothy de la Vina, Gavin Sekhon

Tools

‍Adobe After Effects, Adobe Premiere Pro

Process

We initially came up with a script and storyboard for our documentary. I put together a mood board and one of my team members made a draft storyboard. Based on that storyboard, took B-roll footage at various locations such as recycling depot, parks, transit stations, and downtown areas while waiting for potential interviewees.

Fig 1: Moodboard

During the interview, we used the standard Three-Camera setup to provide a clean and focused shot. I was responsible for the operation of the secondary camera, making sure that the composition of the subject mimics that of the wide shot. The camera was set up in a way to focus on the subject in a wide to medium shot while eliminating the background distractions. The rule of thirds was used to complement the subject's eye line while he was looking towards the main camera. I was also in charge of recording the audio using a lapel mic and Zoom. I ensured that the audio levels were consistent, clear, and within an optimal range.

Fig 2: Three-camera interview setup

After we got all our footage and audio, I first had to sync the audio files to match the interview footage. Using a clapperboard during the interview helped me speed up this process. I then started editing the parts of the whole interview footage and kept the ones that I thought were relevant. I then arranged and sequenced the clips to fit with our narrative. This was the most difficult part as it was a long interview and contained various aspects of the topic. I chose the clips which would emphasize the topic and would create awareness and emotions. After getting feedback from the teaching team as well as the interviewee, we made final edits to the film and one of my team members color-corrected the final footages.

Fig 3: Editing the footages using Adobe Premiere Pro

The biggest challenge that our group faced in this project was having to scrap our project midway through the term. We were told that we would not be working with that organization due to some concerns from the academic side. So, in the remaining few weeks of the class, we had to redraw our storyboard and look for potential interviewees. We also had to retake few B-roll footages which would fit our new narrations, and we worked closely with our professor for feedback and advice.

Reflection

This project was not my first time creating a short film, but this is the first time I had to film a short documentary. Even though some aspects and principles of filming are the same, there are some unique features of a documentary. It is interesting to develop a story and put someone else's opinion to the story to make it more powerful. Especially, since our topic tackles global issues, we need a good narration and a powerful interview. And that is exactly what we achieved. Even though we encountered problems and obstacles such as scraping our whole ideas and had to restart the project halfway through, good teamwork with firm decisions carried us a long way.
